Jasmine Shamnani (Founder)

8th Grader, Marlboro Memorial Middle School, NJ

Every year as I move betwen grades, I have piles of used items like textbooks, games and musical instruments that lie around never to be used again. I wanted to find these items a new home while cashing in some money to help me buy items that I need for my upcoming grade. Yes, I could have sold them to the re-sellers like Amazon, AbeBooks, and GameStop. However, I would only get pennies on the dollar. I saw the opportunity in my K12 community where exchanging these items could benefit both the local buyers and sellers. Buyers gain by purchasing items for cheaper than the re-seller websites. And sellers gain by getting a better price for their items than selling it to a re-seller website. Starting this website is my way of bringing my K12 community closer and mutually benefiting from each other.

Arvin Satwani (Co-Founder)

9th Grader, J.P. Stevens, Edison, NJ

On a casual Saturday morning, Jasmine sold this idea to me. After hearing from her, I realized that I too have a pile of items that I have accumulated over the years and they need TLC. I joined as the Co-Founder to help her with her K12 community at Marlboro NJ, while trying to bring this concept to life in my own K12 community at Edison, NJ
